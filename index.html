Research Portfolio

Projects

Each project opens into a text block with a short explanation, methods, and goals.

A modular implementation path for interstellar medium physics, star formation, and stellar feedback in large hydrodynamical simulation codes. The goal is to preserve legacy models while enabling a cleaner, physically motivated, and production-style alternative path.

This project involves code architecture, snapshot I/O design, chemistry and cooling backends, timestep consistency, MPI-safe workflows, and validation against controlled physical tests.

A research effort focused on extracting orbital, kinematic, and structural diagnostics from simulation snapshots. This includes merger history tracking, pericentric passage identification, gas and stellar mass evolution, and robust visualization of time series.

A project dedicated to studying stellar populations and metallicity structure in the Sagittarius system using narrow-band photometry and complementary dynamical information. This line of work connects observations, stellar population diagnostics, and theoretical expectations from disruption models.

Development of kinetic Monte Carlo tools for charge transport, photogeneration, degradation, memory effects, and time-dependent observables in coarse-grained materials models. The emphasis is on physically interpretable structure, modularity, and reproducible output.
